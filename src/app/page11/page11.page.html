Description -The keys to this species are the segments not round or beadlike, the pinnae scaly, at least on the midribs, and glabrous or nearly so. Rachises are grooved adaxially. Leaves bright green, tripinnate, triangular, smooth and hairless. Dense stands in damp shade in gravel at base of igneous rock outcrops.

Spores -Confined to the submarginal vein tips or scattered along veins near the segment margins, usually protected by the reflexed leaf margins. sporulating late spring-fall

Habitat -usually on igneous substrates, on rocky slopes and ledges, cliff bases, from 2,500-5,000 ft

Range -This species appears to be restricted to the Sonoran Desert
