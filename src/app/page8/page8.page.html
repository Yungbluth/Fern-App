Myriopteris aurea

Golden Cloak Fern

Description - abaxial surface of is covered in dense white hairs. Pinnae appear to have a short petiole, though technically the pinna is simply articulate. Leaf is only modestly dissected into lobed pinnae, which are hairy both above and below. Distinguished from the other Cheilanthes by the scalloped appearance of the blades, rather than having a beadlike appearance, as well as the distinctive dull green above and dense yellowish hairs below.

Spores - sori continuous around margin, partly hidden by dense hairs. sporulates summer-fall.

Habitat - dry ledges and rocky slopes, on a variety of substrates from 4,000-7,000 ft

Range - Ariz., N.Mex., Tex.; Mexico; West Indies; Central America; South America
